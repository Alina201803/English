**Injustice** UK /ɪnˈdʒʌstɪs/ US /ɪnˈdʒʌstɪs/

1. 助记,

   in-(前缀，"不"的意思，  "not, opposite of, without",from PIE root [*ne-](https://www.etymonline.com/word/*ne-?ref=etymonline_crossreference) "not.")+just(源自justus，“公平的，公正的”的意思，from ius "a right," especially "legal right, law")+-ice(名词后缀)

   在古希腊神话里，主持正义和秩序的女神是忒弥斯（Themis），是天神乌拉诺斯和大地女神盖亚所生，十二泰坦神之一，宙斯的第二任妻子。在罗马神话中，正义女神的名字叫做“朱斯提提亚”（Justitia）。她的塑像经常出现在法院中，其形象通常是身披白袍，双眼蒙布，左手提一秤，右手举一剑。白袍象征道德无瑕，刚直不阿；蒙眼代表理性判断，不受感官和人情影响；秤象征公平；剑表示严厉制裁。造像的背面往往刻有古罗马的法谚：“为实现正义，哪怕天崩地裂。”
   传说，有一次众神失和，争论不休，没有人能够进行调解裁决。朱斯提提亚挺身而出，用白布蒙眼，为众神进行裁决。从此以后她就成为了正义女神，专司律法裁决。
   朱斯提提亚的名字Justitia在拉丁语中就是“正义”的意思。英语词根jur-/just-（正义、法律）就来源于此。

2. 词源,

   **OED Origin**

   Late Middle English: from Old French, from Latin injustitia, from in- ‘not’ + justus ‘just, right’.

   **Etymology**

   late 14c., from Old French injustice "unfairness, injustice" (14c.), from Latin iniustitia "unfairness, injustice," from iniustus "unjust, wrongful, unreasonable, improper, oppressive," from in- "not" (see [in-](https://www.etymonline.com/word/in-?ref=etymonline_crossreference) (1)) + iustus "just" (see [just](https://www.etymonline.com/word/just?ref=etymonline_crossreference) (adj.)). Injust (adj.) is attested from late 15c., from French, but [unjust](https://www.etymonline.com/word/unjust?ref=etymonline_crossreference) is the usual English word.

3. 释义,

   **justice**

   n. 1)[U]公平；公正；合理(fairness)**the fair treatment of people；the quality of being fair or reasonable**

   ​     2)[U]司法制度；法律制裁；审判(law) **the legal system used to punish people who have committed crimes**

   ​     3)[C]（称谓）法官；上诉庭法官(judge)**(NAmE)a judge in a court (also used before the name of a judge)；(BrE, CanE)used before the name of a judge in a court of appeal**  

4. 相关词语和词组

   **just** adj.公正的，公平的，合理的，正直的，正义的，正确的

   ​        adv.只是，仅仅，刚刚，正好，恰好，实在 

   **justify** vt.证明……是正确的，替……辩护

   ​             vi.证明合法

   **jury** n.陪审团

   **injure** vt.伤害，损害（对人行不正义之事）

   **injury** n. 伤害，损害；受伤处

   **bring someone to justice** (将某人)绳之以法，缉拿归案

   **do justice to sb/sth; do sb/sth justice** 公平对待某人(或某事)；恰当处理某人(或某事)

   **do oneself justice** 充分发挥自己的能力

   **pervert the course of justice** 做伪证，妨碍司法公正

   **in justice to** 为了对…公平起见；(对某人)做出应有的评价

   **Mr (or Mrs) Justice** 法官先生(或夫人)

   **do yourself/sb an injustice** 待…不公正；冤枉

**Obsolute** UK /ˈæbsəlu:t/ US /ˈæbsəˌlut/

1. 助记,

   ab-(前缀，远离的意思， "off, away from")+solut(solvere，“松开”的意思，"to loosen, untie, release, detach," from PIE *se-lu-)

   字面义“松开而远离”，鲜明的解除束缚、获得自由的过程。由“松开束缚”的具体意思抽象化为“不受条件约束的、无条件的”，无条件的就是绝对的：牛顿理论只适用于宏观，微观解释不了，故它受制于宏观，不是绝对的。

   absolute, absolution, absolve都来自 latin动词absolvere[set free], 由ab- ‘away’ 加上动词solvere [loose] 合成。

   在13-16世纪表示相同意思的assoil用的频率高于absolve。absolve直接来自古法语，但是它们都来自拉丁语。absolute与absolution中的t 来自拉丁动词的过去分词形式absolūt-。absolve现在多用来表示 freeing from responsibility；absolution 表示remitting of sins；absolute 表示freeing from any qualification or restriction。

2. 词源,

   **OED Origin**

   Late Middle English: from Latin absolutus ‘freed, unrestricted’, past participle of absolvere (see absolve).
   **Etymology**

   late 14c., "unrestricted, free from limitation; complete, perfect, free from imperfection;" also "not relative to something else" (mid-15c.), from Latin absolutus, past participle of absolvere "to set free, acquit; complete, bring to an end; make separate," from ab "off, away from" (see [ab-](https://www.etymonline.com/word/ab-?ref=etymonline_crossreference)) + solvere "to loosen, untie, release, detach," from PIE *se-lu-, from reflexive pronoun *s(w)e- (see [idiom](https://www.etymonline.com/word/idiom?ref=etymonline_crossreference)) + root [*leu-](https://www.etymonline.com/word/*leu-?ref=etymonline_crossreference) "to loosen, divide, cut apart."

3. 释义,

   adj. 1)完全的；全部的；绝对的**total and complete**

   ​        2)(英语口语中尤用以强调)道地的，确实的，十足的**used, especially in spoken English, to give emphasis to what you are saying**

   ​        3)肯定的；无疑的；明确的 **definite and without any doubt or confusion**

   ​        4)不受限制的；不受约束的**not limited or restricted**

   ​        5)独立的；绝对的**existing or measured independently and not in relation to sth else**  

   n. 绝对真理（指思想或原理）**an idea or a principle that is believed to be true or valid in any circumstances**

4. 相关词语和词组

   **absolutely** adv. (absolute+ly)

   **absolution** n. (尤指基督教中的)赦罪；赦免

   **absolve** v. 宣告…无罪，赦免…的罪行；免除责任 [义务] ,开脱（罪责）

   **absolute confidence/trust/silence/truth** 充满信心；绝对信任；万籁俱寂；绝对真实

   **absolute power/authority** 无上权力；绝对权威

   **an absolute ruler/monarchy** 独裁统治者；专制君主

   **absolute majority** 绝对多数(指超过半数的选票或竞选席位)



**Approach** v. /səˈspɛkt/ n. adj. /ˈsʌspɛkt/

1. 助记,

   sus(表示“在下，向上”)+spect(v.怀疑；n.被怀疑者，嫌疑犯)

   suspect : [sus-(sub-)=under在……下面；(s)pect=to look 看→“to look at someone from under or askance私下斜眼看某人”→]，与suspicion同义。

   spect，词源同spectate，respect，词根为spek-“to observe”

2. 词源,

   **OED Origin**

   Middle English (originally as an adjective): from Latin suspectus ‘mistrusted’, past participle of suspicere, from sub- ‘from below’ + specere ‘to look’.
   **Etymology**

   early 14c., "suspected of wrongdoing, under suspicion;" mid-14c., "regarded with mistrust, liable to arouse suspicion," from Old French suspect (14c.), from Latin suspectus "suspected, regarded with suspicion or mistrust," past participle of suspicere "look up at, look upward," figuratively "look up to, admire, respect;" also "look at secretly, look askance at," hence, figuratively, "mistrust, regard with suspicion," from assimilated form of sub "up to" (see [sub-](https://www.etymonline.com/word/sub-?ref=etymonline_crossreference)) + specere "to look at" (from PIE root [*spek-](https://www.etymonline.com/word/*spek-?ref=etymonline_crossreference) "to observe"). The notion behind the word is "look at secretly," hence, "look at distrustfully."

3. 释义,

   v. 1)疑有，觉得(尤指坏事可能属实或发生)(think likely)**to think or believe something to be true or probable**

   ​    2)怀疑(某人有罪)(think guilty)**to think that someone has committed a crime or done something wrong**

   ​    3)怀疑；感觉有问题；不信任(doubt)**to not trust; to doubt**

   n. 被怀疑者，嫌疑犯**a person believed to have committed a crime or done something wrong, or something believed to have caused something bad**

   adj. 令人怀疑的，不可信的**possibly false or dangerous**

4. 相关词语和词组

   suspected 1)adj. 令人怀疑的，不可信的

   ​                    2)v.(suspect的过去分词) 怀疑，猜想

   suspectable adj. 可疑的，有嫌疑的，值得怀疑的

   unsuspected adj. 未被怀疑的，无嫌疑的

   unsuspecting 1)adj. 不怀疑的，无猜疑的；可信任的；未料到的，未猜想到的；

   ​                         2)adv. 不怀疑地，无猜疑地；可信任地

   It's suspected that … 有人怀疑

   suspect sb/sth (of doing sth) 怀疑某人做了某事

**Resigned**

1. 助记
  re(前缀，from re- "opposite")+sign(signare "to make an entry in an account book," literally "to mark," from Latin signum "identifying mark, sign")+ed(形容词后缀)
2. 词源
  **OED Origin**
  Late Middle English: from Old French resigner, from Latin resignare ‘unseal, cancel’, from re- ‘back’ + signare ‘sign, seal’.
  **Etymology**
  ate 14c., "give up, surrender, abandon, submit; relinquish," from Old French resigner "renounce, relinquish" (13c.), from Latin resignare "to check off, annul, cancel, give back, give up," from re- "opposite" (see [re-](https://www.etymonline.com/word/re-?ref=etymonline_crossreference)) + signare "to make an entry in an account book," literally "to mark," from Latin signum "identifying mark, sign" (see [sign](https://www.etymonline.com/word/sign?ref=etymonline_crossreference) (n.)).
3. 释义
  adj. 已放弃的；顺从的accepting that something you do not like will happen because you cannot changeit
  adv. 顺从地，听从地
  v. 辞职，放弃(resign的过去式)；(to)使顺从
4. 相关词语和词组
  resignation n.辞职; 顺从; 辞职书; 听从
  resign yourself to something 听任某事
